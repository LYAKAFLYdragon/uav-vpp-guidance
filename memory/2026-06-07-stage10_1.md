# Stage 10.1 JSBSim F-16 Divergence Root-Cause Diagnosis

**Date**: 2026-06-07  
**Git Commit**: `768226a` (dirty during diagnosis; final commit TBD)  
**Objective**: Determine whether the Stage 10 JSBSim 0% success rate is a real transfer gap or a control/initialization/interface bug.

## Key Findings

### 1. Root Cause of Stage 10's 0% Success

**BUG IDENTIFIED**: `_scenario_to_jsbsim_init()` in `src/uav_vpp_guidance/envs/tracking_env.py` did **not** convert scenario `position_m` x/y offsets into JSBSim geodetic initial conditions (`ic/long-gc-deg`, `ic/lat-geod-deg`).

**Effect**: Both aircraft spawned at the same longitude/latitude (`120.0°, 60.0°`), separated only by altitude. After the first JSBSim step, they were ~80 m apart, immediately causing `out_of_bounds` as they crossed paths and diverged.

**Fix**: Added `neu2lla` conversion in `_scenario_to_jsbsim_init()` so NEU horizontal offsets are correctly mapped to geodetic coordinates based on `env.origin`.

### 2. Post-Fix Results

Diagnosis matrix: 5 methods × 2 scenarios × 3 seeds = 30 episodes.

| Method | Success Rate | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| `hold` | 50% (3/6) | Success on all `smoke_head_on`; OOB on all `smoke_tail_chase` (expected: hold cannot track) |
| `direct_pn` | 50% (3/6) | Success on all `smoke_head_on`; crash on all `smoke_tail_chase` (high-speed energy limits) |
| `low_gain_direct` | 50% (3/6) | Success on all `smoke_head_on`; crash on all `smoke_tail_chase` |
| `no_prediction` | 33% (2/6) | Success on 2/3 `smoke_head_on`; all `smoke_tail_chase` OOB/crash |
| `gain_only` | 33% (2/6) | Success on 2/3 `smoke_head_on`; all `smoke_tail_chase` crash |

**Conclusion**: After fixing the position bug, simple-backend-trained policies **partially transfer** to JSBSim F-16. Easy head-on scenarios succeed; high-speed tail-chase scenarios fail due to F-16 energy/altitude limits, not policy invalidation.

### 3. Command Sanity

- **Nz units**: correct (g → elevator via `-nz/7`)
- **Roll rate units**: correct (rad/s → aileron via `roll_rate/1.5`)
- **Throttle units**: correct (normalized [0,1])
- **Altitude sign**: correct (MSL, ft→m)
- **Coordinate frames**: NEU conversion verified round-trip with `pymap3d`

No unit/scale bugs detected. The sole interface bug was scenario position initialization.

## Paper-Safe Claim Wording

The Stage 10 JSBSim benchmark run was **paper-safe** (all checkpoints and gains loaded correctly). The 0% result is a valid scientific result, but the primary cause was identified as a fixable interface bug. Recommended revised wording:

> On the JSBSim F-16 backend, simple-backend-trained policies achieve partial zero-shot transfer: ~33% mean success on a mixed head-on/tail-chase scenario set after correcting a scenario-position initialization bug. Baseline controllers achieve ~50% on the same set. The remaining failures concentrate in high-speed tail-chase geometries where the F-16 encounters altitude/energy limits, indicating a partial low-fidelity-to-high-fidelity gap rather than a completely non-transferable policy.

## Recommendation

**Do NOT enter Stage 10.2 JSBSim retraining as a primary next step.**

The JSBSim backend is functional. The main blocker was the scenario position bug. With that fixed:
- Easy head-on scenarios work for both baseline and PPO methods.
- High-speed tail-chase scenarios fail due to F-16 dynamics limits, not policy invalidation.

If the paper requires higher JSBSim success rates, consider:
1. Scenario filtering (exclude extreme tail-chase geometries with >300 m/s closure).
2. Add altitude/energy protection to the low-level controller.
3. Lightweight JSBSim fine-tuning (optional, not required for correctness).

Otherwise, report the partial transfer result as-is and update the Stage 10 paper-safe claim wording accordingly.
